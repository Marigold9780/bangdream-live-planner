## Chinese Public Holidays Reference

When the user mentions "holiday" or "vacation" without specifics, search for the current year's Chinese public holidays. Common patterns:
- New Year (元旦): January 1
- Spring Festival (春节): Late January/Early February (varies by lunar calendar)
- Qingming (清明): Early April
- Labor Day (劳动节): May 1 and surrounding days
- Dragon Boat (端午): June (lunar)
- Mid-Autumn (中秋): September/October (lunar)
- National Day (国庆节): October 1-7

Use web search with queries like `2026年中国法定节假日安排` to get exact dates.

## Hotel Area & Local Exploration Guide

### Major Live Venue Areas & Nearby Highlights

**Tokyo Garden Theater (有明アリーナ / 有明地区)**
- Area: Ariake / Odaiba
- Nearby attractions: TeamLab Planets, Odaiba Seaside Park, DiverCity Tokyo Plaza (Gundam statue), Palette Town
- Dining: Aqua City Odaiba (mall food court and restaurants), DECKS Tokyo Beach
- Transport: Yurikamome Line or Rinkai Line to Tokyo Big Sight / Ariake stations

**Yokohama Arena**
- Area: Yokohama (Kanagawa)
- Nearby attractions: Yokohama Chinatown, Minato Mirai waterfront, Cosmo World, Red Brick Warehouse
- Dining: Chinatown for authentic Chinese/Japanese-Chinese food; Minato Mirai for waterfront dining
- Transport: JR Keihin-Tohoku Line or Yokohama Municipal Subway to Shin-Yokohama or Kannai

**Zepp DiverCity (Tokyo)**
- Area: Odaiba
- Same as Tokyo Garden Theater area notes above

**Zepp Haneda / Zepp Namba / Zepp Osaka Bayside**
- Consult venue-specific access guides; often in entertainment/commercial complexes

**Nippon Budokan**
- Area: Kudanshita / Imperial Palace East Garden
- Nearby attractions: Imperial Palace East Garden, Yasukuni Shrine, Kitanomaru Park
- Dining: Jimbocho area (old book town with many small restaurants)
- Transport: JR Sobu Line, Tozai Line, Hanzomon Line to Kudanshita

**Saitama Super Arena**
- Area: Saitama (just north of Tokyo)
- Nearby: Cocoon City shopping mall, Railway Museum
- Transport: JR Keihin-Tohoku Line or Utsunomiya Line to Saitama-Shintoshin

### Dining Recommendations by Area

**General Tips**
- Many popular restaurants in Japan do not take reservations for small groups; expect to queue
- Use **Tabelog** (食べログ) to check ratings and reservation availability
- Lunch sets (ランチ) are often significantly cheaper than dinner (50-70% of dinner price)
- Chain restaurants (Saizeriya, Gusto, Matsuya, Sukiya) are budget-friendly and require no reservation
- Convenience stores (Lawson, 7-Eleven, FamilyMart) offer surprisingly good pre-packaged meals and snacks

**Reservation / Queue Strategy**
| Restaurant Type | How to Book | Notes |
|-----------------|-------------|-------|
| High-end / popular | Tabelog (web/app), phone, or hotel concierge | Book 1-2 weeks ahead |
| Mid-range chains | Walk-in or Tabelog | Weekday lunch = less crowded |
| Ramen shops | Ticket machine at entrance | Queue during peak hours (12:00-13:00, 18:00-20:00) |
| Izakaya | Walk-in or phone | Happy hour (17:00-19:00) often has discounts |
| Conveyor belt sushi | Walk-in | Kura Sushi, Sushiro, Hamazushi are budget-friendly |

## Anime Pilgrimage (圣地巡礼) & Meme Landmarks

### Primary Source: anitabi.cn
**URL**: https://www.anitabi.cn/

This is the primary resource for finding real-world locations featured in anime. When the user is staying in or traveling through an area:
1. Visit anitabi.cn and search for the anime title (e.g., "BanG Dream!", "Girls Band Cry")
2. Note the real-world addresses and station names of featured spots
3. Cross-reference with Google Maps to calculate travel time from the user's hotel
4. Present them as P1 priority attractions with context about which scene/episode featured them

### BanG Dream! Franchise Pilgrimage Spots
The BanG Dream! series features many real Tokyo locations:

- **Hanazono Shrine (花園神社)** — Shinjuku; featured in early BanG Dream! promotional material and imagery
- **Shinjuku Alta / Shinjuku Station area** — Appears in various BanG Dream! media
- **Shimokitazawa (下北沢)** — Featured in BanG Dream! It's MyGO!!!!!; known for live music venues and vintage shops
- **Various live houses (ライブハウス)** — Many real small venues in Shimokitazawa, Shibuya, and Koenji appear as inspiration for the series' live house settings
- **Tokyo Big Sight / Ariake area** — Venue for major BanG Dream! events in real life; often referenced

Search strategy: `BanG Dream 圣地巡礼`, `バンドリ 聖地`, `anitabi BanG Dream`

### Girls Band Cry Pilgrimage Spots
Girls Band Cry (トゲナシトゲアリ / Togenashi Togeari) features locations primarily in:

- **Koenji (高円寺)** — Central setting for the anime; many streetscapes and shop exteriors match exactly
- **Shin-Koenji Station area** — Multiple recognizable street corners and storefronts
- **Sub Culture Cafe** — Real cafe in Koenji that inspired the band's practice space
- **Koenji live houses** — Various small music venues in the area inspired the anime's live house
- **Nakano Sun Plaza** — Nearby landmark referenced in the series

Search strategy: `Girls Band Cry 圣地巡礼`, `トゲナシトゲアリ 聖地`, `anitabi Girls Band Cry`, `girls band cry koenji locations`
